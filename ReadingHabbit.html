How to start your reading habit?


Having a reading habit is one of the most well-known routines one tries to accomplish besides workouts, meditation, and healthy eating habits. How often have you made up your mind to start your reading habit? However, some circumstances and distractions made you drift from attaining that goal over and over again. Starting a reading habit looks like a tremendous task, but there are all sorts of tricks to help you with this.

Don’t start with heavy literature.

Choosing books like “To Kill a Mockingbird”, “Crime and Punishment” or “The Stranger” to look like a passionate reader will doom your reading goal. Try to choose books you are interested in before jumping to more serious readings. Try some light reading first. Pop phycology might be a good start if you are not into fiction. Here I can recommend “The subtle art of not giving a f*uck” written by Mark Manson.

Don’t overachieve.

Even if you set up your mind reading a book every week, you already know that will not work out. Instead, try to have some realistic targets. Try with one page a day.

Bunch your habits.

Pair one thing you enjoy with reading. By pairing something you enjoy doing with a new hobby, you will liken pleasure to the new activity. If you like drinking coffee in the morning, try reading one page while enjoying your warm coffee.
Try audiobooks. If you like cooking, jogging, knitting, try to listen to an audiobook simultaneously.

Change that negativity, honey!!

 If you start by thinking that you already have read or that the goal will fail, then be sure that it will fail. Negative thinking is a way of building resistance against a new hobby.

Motivate yourself

Try to motivate yourself with small rewards such as a piece of chocolate, time on social media, video games, or movies.
Get a book buddy. You can find someone who shares the same reading interests as you do. You can share thoughts, ideas, and even recommendations. Moreover, you can motivate each other to read more and to keep up with the new habit.

Find the WHY

Try to identify the reasons why you want to become a reader. This will help you integrate a reading habit and make it an integrate part of your identity.


I will drop here some reading recommendations. These books made me want to continue with reading as a habit. 

1. A man called Ove -  Fredrik Backman
2. The Kite Runner -  Khaled Hosseini
3. Kafka on the Shore - Haruki Murakami
4. Love - Toni Morrison 
5. Paradise -  Toni Morrison
