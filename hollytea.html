Holy Tea

How To Make the Perfect Cup of Tea. 
3 Easy recipes for neophytes

Summertime is the perfect moment of the year for bookworms like us to catch up with the readings. New books drop just in time for our summer vacation, and I am here to give you some perfect tea recipes to match your reading list. Let me show you some perfect mixes to take with you in your reading nuke while you are hiding far from the madding crowd.

I don’t know how many of you have watched the Black Butler, but here is when the madness with the black tea started for me. I used to drink it plain, without anything added. However, this year on a trip to Sibiu, the game changed radically. My friends made me try the black tea together with milk and honey. The combination blew my mind.
Black tea is simple. Here is all you need:

black tea leaves
honey
milk

All you have to do is to boil some water in the kettle, add leaves or teabags. If you want to drink the tea with milk, add a little milk to each cup and mix with a teaspoon of honey.

I usually read art history, biographies, or historical fiction together with this type of tea. Risking to sound bougie, I would like to say that this specific pairing brings a pinch of sophistication to the entire reading process. My fellow lovers of the Dark Academia aesthetic will understand what I mean by that.  

The second one is the Matcha tea. Please be careful with this one because it can turn out bad quite easily.
Don’t panic! I have some tricks for you.

! When you brew the tea make sure that the temperature is approximately 70-80 degrees Celsius. The umami reveals itself when you are brewing the tea at this warm water temperature.

! Make sure you use filtered water. The Japanese people are careful about the water they pour over the tea. Filtered water is  good  with the Yuzamashi technique.

All you must do is boil your filtered water for about 5 minutes and let it cool to 70-80 degrees Celsius, after that you can pour it over your Matcha.

I usually add some almond milk over the foamy Matcha tea in order to make a delicious cup of Matcha Latte. If necessary, add some honey for the sweetness.  

I love to pair this tea type with poetry books and Women's Fiction. Right now, I have The Dutch House on my reading list. I just wait for the exam session to be over so I can have a perfect cup of Matcha tea in my hands together with this book. A review will follow for sure in August, so stay tuned.

The last tea type I am to tell you about today is the well-known iced tea. I love this tea in combination with some light reading such as romances and sci-fi

You can easily prepare one with the following ingredients:
130g raspberries,
80g honey
45g fresh ginger
8 white/jasmine tea bags
3 lemons

I hope that you will enjoy these recipes and that you will have a mellow summer.

Just as Ella Fitzgerald said: Is summertime, and “the livin' is easy”!
