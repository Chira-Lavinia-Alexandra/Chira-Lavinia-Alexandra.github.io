Coffee recipes
for a mellow Sunday morning

Coffee for your reading nook

Have you ever found yourself thinking that you want to prepare more than a simple cup of coffee? I’ve been there too. During the past year, I found some coffee shops, here in Cluj-Napoca that changed my perspective on coffee making.
I want to show you some coffee recipes that I like to prepare when going to my reading nook.

The Flat White

Why is it flat? Why is it white? Isn’t it the same as a latte? – well…. No

After googling this beverage, I discovered that the flat white is from the categories of white coffees like Lattes are. The idea of the flat came, according to the links below, from Australia. It was necessary to distinguish between a latte and the flat white, a coffee with less foam.

The origins of the flat white remains in the fog of modern history because there are numerous people and coffee shops to claim its creation. One story comes from Auckland, New Zealand, where Derek Townsend and Darrell Ahlers from Cafe DKD said that they attempted to make an Italian latte.

Another story comes from Moors Espresso Bar in Sydney.  Please feel free to check the sources I used. I know passionate readers and coffee geeks like you would love to see more on the origin of this coffee recipe.

!!! Please be aware that there is a website dedicated only to THE FLAT WHITE
Enough talk from now. Let’s get to the core.

How do we prepare it?
- 2 shots of espresso
- 120 ml milk

While the espresso is brewing, steam 120 ml of milk to 140°F.

I think I tried an affogato when I had my entrance exam at the university. Having espresso shots combined with vanilla globes felt like the happiest cooking accident ever made.

I'm convinced that Archimedes would yell again "Eureka!!!" naked on the hallways for affogato. I love a good affogato during the hot summer days. Just imagine sitting in a garden, or in a hammock in the park with an affogato in one hand and a book in the other.

How do you prepare it?
- vanilla ice crea
- espresso
- Liqueur/ nuts/ almonds/ aromas (optional)
