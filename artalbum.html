Dalí 

Dalí is the artist that has caught my attention for the past two years. It is still not clear to me if Salvador Dalí was a genius or a madman … probably both. However, I am not here to judge him.

Robert Descharnes and Gilles Neret wrote one of the best books on the art and life of Dalí.

Salvador Domingo Felipe Jacinto Dalí Domenech was born in Figueras in the province of Gerona. The Catalonian coastal strip from Crape Creus will be the scene for many nightmarish visions haunted by anthropomorphic shapes. You will see his evolution from his anti-Faust period to Impressionism, modernism, neo-cubism, cubism, realism, and surrealism. Basically, he tried all the-isms.

You will get a glimpse of all the drama and scandal that surrounded Dalí’s life. Moreover, you will find out that Dalí was “the first painter of behinds in the history of art, and in the history of the human derrière” (Romero quoted in Descharnes, Robert, et al 1993). Paintings get explained through the prism of his life experiences. Food porn and irrational fears of ants, armpits, and women will appear in his later work. Even when reading his autobiographies, you may struggle to grasp some of his ridiculous ideas.

This man is talented, sassy, controversial, and nonetheless a jerk. He literally teased a girl and broke up with her right after only to see her cry and make a painting about the emotion.

Seriously, find this album and read it. The history of this man is intriguing and captivating. I will end this with a quote from the album: “Rarely does a young painter make his appearance on the scene with the audacity and aplomb of Salvador Dalí” (ibidem).
