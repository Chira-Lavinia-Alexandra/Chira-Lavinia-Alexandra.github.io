The joy
behind
the lens

Make silliness your mindset!

I  love the moments behind the camera!

On Instagram you get to see the finite brushed-up product, but you are robbed of all the bloopers. There are award winning cringe moments, jokes, unfriendly weather conditions and waves of silly humor that are not caught by the lens. There are dozens of photos that are never going to be published online. All these “bloopers” might give you an insight into the life of a bookstagramer.

When I started this small project for my Master's program, I decided to set aside the easy way out. Therefore, I decided to be stubborn, take my own photos, and to write my own creative content. I wanted to share with both my fellow readers and the world the books that I was excited about.

skilled boyfriend in action. It's all about that angle

I spent hours in libraries searching the next big read. Hours went by until I finished reading the books. Then the next challenge was to find the perfect background. For my last post, my boyfriend and I went to i Giardini di Zoe in Banpotoc.

We took a backpack full of books, sun screen, fancy glasses, and a bunch of silly jokes and we spent the next three hours taking photos in the garden. There were multiple silly moments, as we both had to wait for various people to move out of the shot, and we strived to find the perfect angle for each book, all the while I was one pall of wind away from a mental breakdown.

The best part was that we had fun. We took silly photos of ourselves and we helped strangers to have nice photos of themselves in the gardens.  All I can say is that I feel blessed to have him as a boyfriend. He was next to me during this entire journey as a neophyte bookstagramer. He helped me set the stage for most of the photos, he shared his knowledge with me, and he was the best Grammar Nazi. 

wind palls became my Achilles' tendon 

I tried to keep my eyes open for the happy mistakes that were ready to unfold before my lens. During these bloopers, I learned that poor color management creates unrealistic photos. I tend to oversaturate some of my photos, and because of this, they look fake and sloppy.

Therefore, in order to avoid these mistakes, I placed my subjects in bright light, while they were surrounded by muted tones (see my posts on A Thousand Splendid Suns and Men Without Women).

I am also guilty of overusing the HDR. I take colors to the extreme. 

Rare capture of my brother being goofy 

I cannot end this article without thanking my brother for his patience with me. At the very beginning of this page, we took five books, and for an entire afternoon, we walked the hills of my village to take some decent shots. In this photo, a storm was coming.

I tried to get a nice photo with the clouds in the background, but my brother decided to have a full Big Foot moment. I cannot utter how happy I am to have this shot of him in my gallery.

I am not the most talented photographer, but I cannot express the joy I felt while using my photos for Instagram posts. It helped me share with my fellow readers how I saw the books and I could express ideas that went beyond words.
